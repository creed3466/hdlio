## Per-algo cluster

| algo       | n | min    | median | mean   | max    |
|------------|---|-------:|-------:|-------:|-------:|
| fast_lio2  | 9 | 0.1714 | 0.2336 | 0.3489 | 1.1822 |
| point_lio  | 9 | 0.1661 | 0.2857 | 0.3568 | 1.0387 |
| ig_lio     | 9 | 0.1656 | 0.2599 | 0.3113 | 0.8084 |

All three baselines cluster tightly. `ig_lio` is marginally the strongest both
on median (0.260 m) and on worst-case (0.808 m). `fast_lio2` has the widest
spread — Varying-illu03 dominates its mean at 1.18 m. `point_lio` sits in
between.

The Mid-360 cluster (~0.17-0.40 m on the well-behaved sequences) sits roughly
at 1.5-2× the corresponding Avia FAST-LIO2 cluster (~0.14 m on Dark01) — within
the soft sanity band (5× ≈ 0.9 m) used as the gate. Mid-360 is a shorter-range
sensor (≈ 70 m vs Avia's ~450 m) so this offset is the expected platform-level
penalty, not a bug in the harness.

## Per-sequence notes

- **Varying-illu03 (17 min, 14 GB bag)** — the only seq where any algo
  exceeds 0.9 m. All three are in the same band (0.81-1.18 m) which suggests a
  shared difficulty of the trajectory (long duration, repeated illumination
  sweeps, possibly degenerate corridors) rather than a per-algo bug. `ig_lio`
  handles it best, `fast_lio2` worst.
- **Occlusion03** — `point_lio` (0.31 m) clearly best; `fast_lio2` (0.43 m) and
  `ig_lio` (0.40 m) closely matched. The other "occlusion" seq (Occlusion04)
  shows the inverse pattern — `point_lio` worst (0.39 m), `ig_lio` best
  (0.21 m). Occlusion handling is sequence-specific, not algorithm-monotone.
- **Dark01 / Dynamic03 / Dynamic04** — all three algos converge to ~0.16-0.22 m,
  the easy-end cluster. Smoke-test value (FAST-LIO2 Dark01 = 0.175 m) reproduced
  exactly under harness conditions.

## Caveats

1. **No paper reference matrix** — M3DGR Mid-360 has no published Tier-1
   benchmark we have located. The 0.17-0.40 m band is internally consistent
   across three algorithms but is not externally validated. Resolution requires
   sourcing the dataset paper or the M3DGR authors' own evaluations.
2. **GT frame assumption** — `compute_ate.py` applies Umeyama alignment with no
   per-pose lever-arm (auto-detected: surfel_data is not NTU VIRAL). This
   assumes the GT and SLAM-output frames coincide modulo a rigid transform.
   If M3DGR distributes GT in a non-body frame (e.g. prism or marker centroid),
   ATE values shift — but the *relative* ranking across algos is preserved.
3. **SLICT and LIO-SAM deferred** — the namespace-only ShapeShifter republisher
   does not satisfy CustomMsg → PointCloud2 conversion. Re-introducing them
   needs a small `livox_to_pointcloud2` adapter (Phase 3 P3-B candidate).
4. **Determinism not separately re-verified** — Phase 2 SOP (multi-run CV) was
   not exercised on Mid-360 in this matrix. The single-run ATE values above
   carry the unstated assumption that Mid-360 satisfies the same `-r 1.0`
   bit-identical guarantee that Avia does. If this becomes load-bearing, run
   `bash docker/run_avia_debug_dark01.sh` analogue for Mid-360 Dark01.
